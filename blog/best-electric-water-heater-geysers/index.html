Buying Guide for Water Heater & Geysers

 

What is an Electric Water Heater (Geyser)?

In simple words, an Electric Geyser uses an energy source for heating water. The process is not much different from a cheap immersion rod, but its a more automatic and safer way of heating water.
The Principle on which it works is that there is a tank with two pipes, one for water inlet another water outlet. The thermostatic heating elements are inside that Tank. It converts the electrical energy into heat through these installed elements.

Get a brief idea about how a water heater and technology behind geysers.

 

Types of Electric Water Heaters

Electric water heater or Geysers are available in the marketplace with a lot of choices, but broadly they are divided into Instant Geyser or Storage Geyser.

1. Instant Geyser

The electric instant geysers have small storage capacity 1-3 liters, and any geyser that can store more than 3 liters comes under electric storage geysers. These are made of a small stainless steel tank that has metallic coils for heating the water. The instant geysers always give you hot water instantly, just like immersion rods but safer way to heat water.

2. Storage Geysers

As the name suggests, a storage water heater (geysers) stores heated water for a specific period. It uses a thermostat to convert cold water to hot water. It uses stainless steel and copper that are expensive but requires less maintenance, whereas the copper ones last longer. The advantage of a storage geyser is that it provides hot water without any breaks.

 

Things to consider while buying the best Electric Water Heater (Geysers)

Some important tips to consider before you buy an essential purchase like buying an electric water heater (Geyser) for your home are :

1. Family Size & capacity

It’s necessary to choose a heater according to the family size and number of members. The capacity of a water heater will depend upon how much water is consumed in a house, for example, a smaller family of just two members, an instant water heater will do the job just fine

2. Installation

The process of installing a water heater is always a headache, so its best to choose the one that offers easy installation or after-sale service.

3. Energy-efficient

An electric water heaters are energy efficient with star rating labels, so choose the one with the highest star rating that is more energy-efficient. It’s the best way to save your money on your electricity bill that goes high in the winter season with the usage of room heaters and water heaters.
Space of the bathroom- The modern water heaters take less space in your restroom than the conventional ones that are bulky. A tank-less water heater also requires lesser space, so for small size bathroom, its best to use the tankless ones.

4. Durability

A water heater built with durable material lasts more than 10 to 15 years. The built-up should be anti-corrosive if it is a tank heater as its subject to decay sooner than a tankless water heater.

5. Additional features

Some water heaters nowadays come with some other features that make them more user-friendly and best choices like auto-off or safety features like deducting any leakage or overheating for safety.
